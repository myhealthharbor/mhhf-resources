🙋👐 For Patient & Caregiver
WHO IS THIS FOR?
This plan is for lesbian, gay, bisexual, transgender, queer, and questioning people and their families and allies. LGBTQ+ identity is not a mental health condition — and was removed from diagnostic manuals decades ago. Minority stress, discrimination, and lack of acceptance cause elevated mental health risk: LGBTQ+ youth attempt suicide at 4x the rate of peers, transgender people face especially high risk. Affirming care dramatically reduces these risks. Family acceptance reduces suicide risk by 50%+. This plan supports affirming mental health care, addresses minority stress, and helps families be allies.

🛡️ Crisis Safety Planning
🛡️ My Crisis Safety Plan
Fill this out NOW — when you are well — so it is ready when you need it. Based on the Stanley-Brown Safety Planning Intervention.
LGBTQ+ youth and trans people have especially elevated suicide risk. Safety plan addresses minority stress crisis, family rejection, discrimination triggers, and accessing LGBTQ+-affirming crisis support.
Step 1 — My personal warning signs (thoughts, feelings, situations that come BEFORE a crisis)
Step 2 — Coping strategies I can do alone (without contacting anyone)
Step 3 — People who provide distraction (places I can go where I do not have to talk about my feelings)
Step 4 — People I can ask for help (3 trusted people with phone numbers)
Step 5 — Professionals I can contact
Step 6 — Making my environment safer (reduce access to means of harm)
Step 7 — My reasons for living (what matters most)
📌 Print this and keep it on your fridge, in your wallet, and saved on your phone. Share with your treatment team and trusted person.

🌟 Beyond the Basics — What Often Gets Missed
🌸 Trauma-Informed Approach
Understanding how past trauma affects current symptoms and healing
▼
LGBTQ+ minority trauma is real trauma. Conversion therapy, family rejection, discrimination, hate violence, and pervasive microaggressions cause real wounds. Trauma-informed care recognizes minority trauma. Religious trauma from non-affirming faith communities is also treatable.
🕊️ Cultural & Faith Considerations
Mental health care that respects your culture, faith, and identity
▼
LGBTQ+ identity exists across all cultures and faiths. Some communities are more accepting than others. Affirming faith communities exist in most traditions. Cultural and racial intersection matters — LGBTQ+ people of color face compounded minority stress. Find providers who understand intersectional identity.
💵 Insurance & Financial Navigation (USA)
Getting affordable care — Medicaid, Medicare, ACA, sliding scale clinics, free options
▼
ACA prohibits discrimination based on sexual orientation/gender identity in health coverage. Most insurance now covers gender-affirming care (varies). Trans Health Project helps with insurance battles. GLMA, WPATH for provider directories. Many community centers offer sliding scale services.
🌙 Sleep & Circadian Rhythm
Why sleep matters more than almost anything else — and how to fix it
▼
Minority stress, hypervigilance, and discrimination affect sleep. Address sleep proactively. Trauma can disrupt sleep severely.
🌱 Gut-Brain Connection & Nutrition
How food, microbiome, and inflammation affect mental health
▼
Stress affects gut. Higher rates of disordered eating in LGBTQ+ community, especially trans people. Address proactively.
🤝 Peer Support — Why It Matters & How to Find It
Connecting with others who get it — the underrated foundation of recovery
▼
LGBTQ+ community is itself protective. PFLAG for families. Trevor Project for youth. SAGE for older adults. Trans Lifeline. Local LGBTQ+ centers. Online communities can be lifesaving for isolated people.
📜 Psychiatric Advance Directive (PAD)
Decide your care while well — to be honored if you cannot speak for yourself
▼
Advance directives critical for LGBTQ+ people — to ensure chosen family/partner has decision-making, not non-affirming biological family. Healthcare proxy, durable POA, will all important. Lambda Legal has resources.

📚 References & Further Reading
The following peer-reviewed studies support the guidance in this care plan. Each includes a plain-English summary.
1. Meyer IH (2003). Prejudice, social stress, and mental health in lesbian, gay, and bisexual populations. Psychol Bull, 129(5):674-697.
What this means for you: Foundational minority stress theory — discrimination causes mental health disparities.
2. Ryan C et al (2010). Family acceptance in adolescence and the health of LGBT young adults. J Child Adolesc Psychiatr Nurs, 23(4):205-213.
What this means for you: Family acceptance reduces suicide attempts by over 50%.
3. Bauer GR et al (2015). Intervenable factors associated with suicide risk in transgender persons. BMC Public Health, 15:525.
What this means for you: Identity documents, social transition, support reduce suicide risk.
4. Russell ST, Fish JN (2016). Mental health in lesbian, gay, bisexual, and transgender (LGBT) youth. Annu Rev Clin Psychol, 12:465-487.
What this means for you: LGBTQ+ youth at higher risk; affirming environments protective.
5. Tordoff DM et al (2022). Mental health outcomes in transgender and nonbinary youths receiving gender-affirming care. JAMA Netw Open, 5(2):e220978.
What this means for you: Gender-affirming care improves mental health.
⚠️ IMPORTANT DISCLAIMER
This care plan is for educational purposes only. It is NOT a substitute for professional mental health care, diagnosis, or treatment. Always work with a licensed mental health professional (psychiatrist, psychologist, therapist, social worker, or counselor) for diagnosis and personalized treatment planning.

If you are in crisis: Call or text 988 (USA Suicide & Crisis Lifeline) immediately. If you have an immediate safety emergency, call 911 (USA), 999 (UK), 000 (Australia), or 112 (Europe). You can also go to any hospital emergency room.

Mental health conditions are real medical conditions — not character flaws or weaknesses. Seeking help is a sign of strength. Recovery is possible. Most people with mental illness lead full, meaningful lives with proper treatment and support.

My Health Harbor Foundation is a nonprofit educational organization. We do not provide direct mental health care, prescribe medications, or replace your treatment team.
